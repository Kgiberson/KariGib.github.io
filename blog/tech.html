Ruby's on...what?

My first-ever glance at Rails

March 10, 2016

What is Rails?

Rails is an opinionated web application development framework, written in Ruby. Rails streamlines the process of making web applications by making assumptions about what every developer needs to get started with their app. While there might be multiple ways to accomplish a goal in Rails, the software makes the assumption that there is a "best" way, aka "the Rails way." - many many ways to do web. Model View Control - (MVC) - tells you where your files should be and their format Why? productivity maintainability - convention for everything Even if you can't use Rails, it's worth learning because you will learn best practice via the path of least resistance, then be able to apply it at your day job

What are the guiding principles of Rails?

  • Don't Repeat Yourself

  • Convention > Configuration

    Why learn Ruby on Rails?

    Rails is founded on pragmatism and established paradigms instead of exotic new ideas. Programmer productivity was the main goal during Rails' development
